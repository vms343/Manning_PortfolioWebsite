About Manning

Manning Farnsworth Snyder is the Chief Operating Officer of NYU's Journal of Politics and International Affairs, an Editor on NYU's Undergraduate Law Review, an Editorial Sports Writer for ESPN Coastal, and the college football beat writer for The Citadel Athletics Department. For the past two summers, Manning was a Teaching Assistant (TA) at Columbia University, where he helped teach classes on "Human Rights" and "Legal Reasoning." He was a Guest Writer for the University of Chicago Undergraduate Law Review and a “Proofreader” at Columbia University in their School of Professional Studies. Manning's work has been featured on CooperSquared.com, CitadelSports.com, and in NYU's Journal of Politics and International Affairs.

Manning is a student at New York University, majoring in Journalism and Sociology. Originally from Charleston, South Carolina, Manning spent his freshman year of college in Paris, France at NYU Paris. During his time in Paris, Manning was the Editor-in-Chief of the NYU Paris Student Newspaper, where he was in charge of the day-to-day and publication responsibilities of the paper. He enjoys writing about politics, sports, and current events, as well as dabbling in speechwriting and copy editing. Manning is passionate about First Amendment law and the intersection between law and media, but is always looking to write on a wide-range of topics.

Manning has been on the Dean's List every year of college and he won the César Award for academic excellence and civic spirit at NYU Paris. He was selected to participate in the NYU Leadership Honors class, an invitation-only course featuring top students from each department. In his free time, Manning can be found playing basketball and chess, reading, or watching (any and all) sports.
